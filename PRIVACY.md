# Privacy Policy — Substack to PDF

**Last updated:** February 24, 2026

## Overview

Substack to PDF is a Chrome extension that converts Substack articles into printer-friendly PDFs. It operates entirely within your browser and does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.

## Data Collection

This extension does **not** collect any data of any kind. Specifically:

- No personal information is collected
- No browsing history is recorded
- No analytics or tracking is used
- No data is sent to any external server
- No cookies are created or read

## How the Extension Works

When you click the extension on a Substack article page, it:

1. Reads the article content (title, author, date, body) from the current tab
2. Temporarily stores this content in your browser's local extension storage
3. Opens a new tab to render the article in a print-friendly format
4. Immediately deletes the temporary data from local storage

All processing happens locally in your browser. No data leaves your device.

## Permissions

The extension requests the following permissions, used solely for the described purposes:

- **activeTab** — To read article content from the current Substack page when you click the extension
- **scripting** — To run a content extraction script on the active Substack page
- **storage** — To temporarily pass extracted article data to the print preview page

## Third Parties

This extension does not use any third-party services, APIs, or analytics. No data is shared with any third party.

## Changes

If this privacy policy is updated, the changes will be reflected in this document with an updated date.
